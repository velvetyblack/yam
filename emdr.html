Bilateral

A moving point and alternating tones, left and right. Sets run ~30 seconds, then stop so you can notice what's present. Tap to begin the next one when you're ready.

For audio, use earbuds. Landscape gives a wider sweep.

A self-guided session

Choose a moderate memory, not your most overwhelming — solo, you're your own container. Keep sets brisk while processing, slower and softer when settling.

Before — set the target

  1. Image — the single worst snapshot.
  2. Negative belief it gives you ("it was my fault," "I'm not safe").
  3. Positive belief you'd rather hold ("I did what I could").
  4. Feeling + body — the emotion, and where it sits physically.
  5. Distress 0–10 — your before-number.

Pick a calm place too — a scene you can return to. That's your off-ramp.

The set loop

Hold the image, the belief, and the body feeling together. Begin a set and follow along — don't steer; let whatever comes, come. At the pause, ask "what do I get now?" and notice what's most present. That becomes the next set.

Continue. Every few sets, return to the target and re-rate 0–10. Watch it come down. Stop when it feels roughly neutral or clearly stops shifting.

When it goes quiet — install

Hold the now-neutral memory with your positive belief; run a few sets letting it feel truer. Then a body scan: sweep head to toe and run a set on anything still tight.

Close — every time

Never get up still activated. If stirred, picture packing what's unfinished into a container with a lid, then sit in your calm place. Ground before standing: feet on floor, name what you see and hear, slow breaths.

If you flood or feel like you're floating out of the present, stop the sets and orient to the room. That's not failure — it's the right move.
